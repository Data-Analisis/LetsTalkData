Open Government Data (OGD)

What is Open Government Data?

"Open Government Data (OGD) is a philosophy- and increasingly a set of policies - that promotes transparency, accountability and value creation by making government data available to all. Public bodies produce and commission huge quantities of data and information. By making their datasets available, public institutions become more transparent and accountable to citizens. By encouraging the use, reuse and free distribution of datasets, governments promote business creation and innovative, citizen-centric services." OECD

“Open data and content can be freely used, modified, and shared by anyone for any purpose” The Open Definition
